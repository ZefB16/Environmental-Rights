EPA – Environmental Protection Agency (USA)

Source: World Book Advanced (Thornton, 2025)

The EPA enforces environmental standards, conducts research, and implemented landmark acts like the Clean Air and Clean Water Acts. These have shaped the nation’s environmental care and policy reforms.

“The agency helps the Council on Environmental Quality develop environmental protection policies and recommend them to the president.”

Relevance: Shows how a national agency ensures accountability in businesses and homes.

Credibility: Expert-authored content from the trusted World Book Encyclopedia.

Paris Agreement – International Climate Cooperation

Source: World Book Advanced (Adams, 2025)

This agreement aims to limit global warming to below 2°C. Although not legally binding, it relies on transparency and peer accountability.

“The goal of the Paris Agreement is to keep global temperatures well within 3.6 °F (2.0 °C)...”

Relevance: Demonstrates global teamwork and accountability in emissions management.

Credibility: World Book provides reliable, neutral information on global policies.

UNDP – Human Rights and Environmental Justice

Source: United Nations Development Programme, 2022

This article addresses the triple planetary crisis and how environmental degradation impacts human rights, especially in marginalized communities.

“The triple planetary crisis of climate change, pollution, and biodiversity loss threatens to reverse decades of human development progress...”

Relevance: Connects environmental and human rights, urging shared responsibility.

Credibility: A publication backed by UN data and principles.

Conclusion: Shared Responsibility

These sources highlight a multi-level effort for sustainability: national laws (EPA), global cooperation (Paris Agreement), and human rights frameworks (UNDP). They emphasize the importance of government policy, ethical business conduct, and individual advocacy in shaping a sustainable future.
